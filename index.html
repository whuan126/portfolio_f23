About Me

Welcome to my portfolio! I'm a fourth-year Computer Science student at UCR with a passion for software development and a knack for turning ideas into reality. Using my strong foundation in object based programming, I've had the privilege of gaining hands-on experience during my 11-month tenure at UCR's Brain Game Center (BGC).

During my time at BGC, I had the opportunity to work on a project that pushed the boundaries of virtual reality technology. My main focus was the development of a Spatial Release application in Unity. Using the Meta Quest 2 as a vessel, I helped a team create a visually and audibly entrancing virtual reality environment.

Working directly under the guidance of two senior developers, I immersed myself in a world of cutting-edge software development practices. Their mentorship was invaluable as I learned to work in a team, implement feedback, and create insightful documentation. My specific role involved designing and implementing massively scalable input systems within the virtual reality environment, accomodating a staggering array of over thirty possible inputs.

When I'm not at my computer, you'll find me in the kitchen, cooking up an insatiable feast for me and my roommates. Otherwise, I'll be hanging out with my house cat, Ron.
